Why Weren't Users Deleting Expired Tags?

Qualitative research that uncovered a behavioral conflict between physical workflows and digital tooling — and directly shaped product roadmap decisions.

Context

A critical feature with near-zero adoption

Our restaurant management platform included a Monitoring Feature designed to help staff comply with health regulations by tracking and deleting expired food tags. On paper, it was a key product differentiator. In practice, our data showed almost no one was using it.

My role: UX researcher and designer. I led a qualitative study to understand why users were abandoning the feature, what alternative behaviors had replaced it, and what would need to change to make it genuinely useful.

The problem

Users weren't ignoring the feature — they'd built their own system

Instead of using the Monitoring Feature, restaurant staff had developed a manual routine called "Bater Praça" — a daily physical walkthrough of every workstation to check and discard expired tags by hand. It was fast, familiar, and required no software.

Staff relied on physical walkthroughs of workstations instead of the digital tool — a habit so ingrained it had a name.

Some users reprinted tags for food they considered still "good," bypassing expiration logic entirely.

This created a real business risk: if users didn't see value in the Monitoring Feature, one of the product's main differentiators was effectively invisible.

Research approach

Qualitative research across three user segments

Since the problem was rooted in behavioral habits rather than usability alone, I used a qualitative approach: stakeholder interviews to align on business assumptions, user interviews and contextual inquiry to observe real workflows, usability testing to identify friction in task completion, and thematic analysis to surface patterns.

I recruited five restaurant employees across three groups:

Never used the feature

1 participant — unaware of its existence or saw no need for it.

Tried it and stopped

2 participants — encountered friction or reverted to manual routines.

Active users

2 participants — used the feature regularly but still faced minor usability challenges.

Findings

Four behavioral profiles, one core tension

Analysis identified four distinct user groups based on their relationship with the feature and their expiration management habits:

Unaware

Had no knowledge of the feature. Relied entirely on Bater Praça.

Disengaged

Tried the feature but found it slower than their physical routine. Some reprinted tags to avoid discarding food.

Inconsistent

Used the feature occasionally but forgot about it — no reinforcement, unclear instructions.

Habitual

Understood the value and built a routine around it. Still encountered friction in the deletion flow.

Key insight

The conflict between manual and digital tracking

Bater Praça was localized and immediate. The Monitoring Feature asked users to do more work for the same outcome — at a workstation level they already trusted.

Tracking every tag from every station inside the software felt redundant and time-consuming when staff already had a fast, embodied routine in place. The feature wasn't solving a problem users felt they had.

The barrier wasn't discoverability or UI — it was a fundamental mismatch between the product's mental model and users' real-world workflows. Any redesign would need to fit into Bater Praça rather than replace it.

Impact

Research directly shaped roadmap decisions

Findings were presented to stakeholders and moved the conversation from "why isn't this feature being used?" to "how do we make this feature fit how users actually work?"

QR code deletion — shipped

A direct outcome of this research: the team prioritized a QR code scan flow that let staff discard expired tags during their physical walkthrough, without switching contexts or navigating the software. The solution was implemented.

Users don't resist features — they resist workflows that don't match their reality. The most effective solution wasn't a better interface. It was meeting users at the moment their existing habit was already happening.
