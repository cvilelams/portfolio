Expired Tags Research

A discovery sprint to understand why expired shelf tags kept breaking downstream workflows.

Context

Stores reported recurring issues when tags expired before replenishment — leading to mis-picks, write-offs, and heavy support load. Leadership asked for root causes before funding a full build.

Problem

Symptoms were visible in tickets, but causes were fragmented across POS, back-office, and manual overrides. No shared definition of “expired” existed between store and HQ teams.

Process

Twelve stakeholder interviews, store visits, and support log clustering. We built a service blueprint and tested three intervention hypotheses with paper prototypes and task scenarios.

Solution

Recommended a phased roadmap: (1) clearer expiry states in the tag UI, (2) automated alerts tied to inventory rules, (3) manager override audit trail. Design paired each phase with measurable ticket and shrink KPIs.

40%

Fewer expired-tag support tickets in pilot stores (90 days)

Outcome

Phase 1 shipped to 20 pilot locations. Product org adopted the research template for other ops investigations. Full build prioritized on the next planning cycle.
